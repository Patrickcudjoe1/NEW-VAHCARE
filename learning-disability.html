UNDERSTANDING

What is a Learning Disability?

A lifelong condition that affects how someone processes, understands and communicates information.

Different for Everyone

Learning disabilities exist on a spectrum. Some people may need minimal support, while others require more comprehensive assistance.

Not About Intelligence

People with learning disabilities often have many strengths and talents. They may excel in creative pursuits or have exceptional social skills.

Lifelong but Manageable

While learning disabilities do not go away, the right support can help individuals develop skills and build confidence.

Can Occur Alongside Other Conditions

Some people may have Autism, Down Syndrome or other conditions alongside their learning disability, requiring specialized understanding and support approaches.

What is the Right Level of Care?

We believe the right level of care is the minimum amount of support needed to help someone achieve maximum independence and life satisfaction.

  • Person-Centred Planning
  • Strength-Based Assessment
  • Environmental Considerations
  • Flexible and Evolving

Levels of Support

Minimal Support

Light-touch assistance for highly independent individuals who need occasional guidance or companionship.

Moderate Support

Regular assistance with specific activities while maintaining overall independence.

Intensive Support

Comprehensive daily support while still promoting choice and self-determination.
